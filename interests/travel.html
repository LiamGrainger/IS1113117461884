Italy

Since a young age, I have been visitng various cities in Italy with my family. The likes of Florence, Rome, Venice, Verona, Lake Garda and more have always provided valuable memories.Home to many of the world's greatest works of art, architecture and gastronomy, Italy elates, inspires and moves like no other.With 48.6 million tourists a year (2014), Italy is the fifth most visited country in international tourism arrivals. People mainly visit Italy for its rich culture, cuisine, history, fashion and art, its beautiful coastline and beaches, its mountains, and priceless ancient monuments. Italy also contains more World Heritage Sites than any other country in the world. Tuscany will always be a fond favourite of mine. This area is possibly the most visited in Italy and contains many popular attractions as well as sought-after landscapes. Rome boasts the remaining wonders of the Roman Empire and some of the world's best known landmarks such as the Colosseum. Florence, regarded as the birthplace of the Italian Renaissance, is Tuscany's most visited city, whereas nearby cities like Siena, Pisa, and Lucca also have rich cultural heritages.



New York City

When I first visited New York City in the Spring of 2015, my expectations (fueled largely by Hollywood blockbusters) were definitely met, and more. Epicenter of the arts. Dining and shopping capital. Trendsetter. New York City wears many crowns, and spreads an irresistible feast for all. Whether it's your first visit or your 50th, New York City is a great destination for a weekend trip. See the landmark sights, catch a Broadway show or explore small, off-the-beaten-path museums like the Lower East Side Tenement Museum. Just as it has activities to suit every interest, New York City has hotel options to suit every pocket. Whether you're looking for a hip new hotel, an old standard or a budget inn, you'll have plenty of options. “One belongs to New York instantly, one belongs to it as much in five minutes as in five years.” ― Tom Wolfe



London

London has alwyas been a city that has captivated me. Whether it's an Autumn stroll along the Embankment, a stroll in Hyde Park or listening to the buskers in Covent Garden, London always has plenty to offer. However for me, Claridges is the quintessence of this city and embodies everything that makes both the city and Britain great. As Jack Wills may say, they're "fabulously British".Prepared and served fresh each day, their menu faithfully follows the traditional combination of sweet and savoury. The ingredients include British specialities such as poached Scottish salmon and their pastries change to reflect the fruits of the season. Of course, at the heart of the experience is the drink itself. From a rich Oolong to the refreshingly complex Claridge’s Blend, you will find teas that complement your food perfectly. Whether it's the Tate or Tussauds, Hyde Park or Harrods, London has something for everyone, from history and culture to fine food and good times.
