Voluntary Work

Organisation: Cork Penney Dinners

Role: Kitchen Staff & Runner

Duration: 2014 - Present

Job Description: Since I was given the opporutnity in Transition Year of Secondary School, I have been working as a volunteer with Cork Penney Dinenrs. Cork Penny Dinners is a warm dry place in the heart of Cork City with a welcoming atmosphere. The premises is open to all 7 days a week all year round, No questions are asked, no judgements are made. All their services are free of charge to those that need them and their friendly volunteers ensure that their service users are well looked after on a daily basis. Their door is always open, they never judge, they serve. Cork Penny Dinners is currently serving up to 2000 freshly made meals per week at our premises compared to approximately 150 or less per week prior to the recession. Sometimes a cup of tea, a handshake or a friendly chat can go a long way towards beating the loneliness and isolation that is a big issue for many that come to the Cork Penney Dinner's door. There has also been a huge increase in homelessness and mental health issues that are affecting so many people which have forced them to avail of our services, most notably in the last few years,with a big increase in the number of families availing of our service. My fellow volunteers come from all walks of life; nurses, truck drivers,scientists, general operatives, musicians, teachers, students, solicitors, unemployed people, retired people and clergy. We are made up of many different nationalities, but what we all have in common is empathy for our fellow human beings and a willingness to offer a hand in support. Service users and volunteers at Cork Penny Dinners interact with good humour and mutual respect always. Since taking up the position, my volunteer work has provided me with a deeper understanding of the plight of those less fortunate than myself, and in turn has made me more grateful and appreciative for what I've often taken for granted.

Role: Activity Staff Leader

Job Description: The Mardyke Arena have a range of activities to suit children of all ages and levels. We welcome over 6000 children into our centre every year through swim lesson programmes, school tours, club and group bookings and multi activity camps. Quality delivery of our programmes and the health and safety of our patrons are our main objectives when it comes to children.Since the Summer of 2015, I have been working on a part-time basis at the Maryke Arena in the centre of Cork City. I, along with a number of my colleagues form the Activity Staff of the Mardyke Arena who cater for Birthday Parties at weekends,Children's Activity Camps which take place during the holiday periods of the primary school calendar, as well as School Tours which take place between April and June of the Calendar year. The Activity Camp programme last for a ten week period during Summer, with two weeks at Easter, and Mid-Term camps in February and October. Just like School Tourss and Birthday Parties, Activity Camps comprise of a 08:00 - 17:30 scheduale, involving various activities and exercises tailored for the child's enjoyment. It is the duty of myself and my colleagues to ensure that each child has an enjoyable, memorable, but most importantly, a safe stay at the Mardyke Arena
